Llamas vs. Alpacas

Llamas

  • Llamas communicate with each other by humming.
  • Llamas have a soothing aura about them and can be trained as professional therapy animals.
  • Llama spit to establish a pecking order, but they rarely spit on humans.
  • Llamas are friendlier to humans than alpacas.
  • Llama poop, which we all know has no odor, also is a great fertilizer and fire starter.
  • Llamas are on average between 5 ft 6 in and 5 ft 9 in, close in height to an average human, so llamas would make a great opponent in a staring contest.
  • When one llama is angry at another, they will stick out their tongue to express their dislike.

  • No, llamas do not lay eggs.

Alpacas

  • Alpacas hum to communicate just like alpacas, as do many livestock.
  • Alpacas are not frequently used as therapy animals or are known for their soothing aura.
  • Alpaca also will spit.
  • Alpacas are more aggresive than llamas.
  • Similarly, alpaca poop does not smell and is nutrient rich, making it a great fertilizer and fire starter.
  • Alpaca are way shorter than llamas! They average about 2.7 - 3.2 ft tall, making them great for staring contents with small children.
  • Animals in the camelid family (both alpaca and llama) stick out their tongue to express annoyance.
  • Alpacas do not feature in either Emperor's New Groove or The Godfather I. However, an alpaca wool rug features in one scene in the film American Gangster.
  • The alpaca wool rug in American Gangster certainly has no speaking roles. It needs to be cleaned.
  • Alpaca are mammals, they also do not lay eggs.
